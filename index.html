About Me

Greetings! I am a PhD student in Computer Science at UC Davis, under the guidance of Professor Prem Devanbu. My research interests encompass Machine Learning for Software Engineering (ML4SE), Naturalness of Software, and Sentiment Analysis. I am highly intrigued by the use of Large Language Models in Software Engineering and devote a significant amount of time to studying them and exploring ways to enhance their safety. I am privileged to have been bestowed with the prestigious Dean's Distinguished Graduate Fellowship (DDGF) for a duration of five years. The Office of Graduate Studies, The College of Engineering, and The Graduate Group in Computer Science at UC Davis have conferred this fellowship upon me in recognition of my exceptional academic performance and potential for productive scholarship. Additionally, I have had the opportunity to work as a summer intern at Microsoft Research in 2022. I completed my Bachelor of Science (B.Sc.) and Master of Science (M.Sc.) in Computer Science and Engineering at Bangladesh University of Engineering and Technology (BUET) in 2014 and 2016, respectively. During my Master's degree, I focused on analyzing the sentiment of peer code reviews and its influence on project outcomes. Prior to my time at Davis, I also served as a faculty member at the Department of Computer Science and Engineering (CSE) in BUET, Dhaka, Bangladesh. My enthusiasm for teaching and research remains a driving force in my professional pursuits.
